## 1. Core truth
This hero video must communicate one single truth:

> **Even when nobody in the business can answer the phone right now, the customer is not left hanging. Their issue is captured, they receive reassurance, and the business stays in flow.**

The viewer must **not** come away thinking:
- nice app
- nice dashboard
- lots of features
- good software

The viewer must come away thinking:

> **Even if nobody there can answer right now, the customer does not get left hanging.**

That is the core job of the hero video.

---

## 2. Psychological target
This video is for local trades businesses:
- very small teams
- small and mid-sized teams
- larger service businesses
- plumbing
- heating
- electrical
- ventilation / climate
- other service-oriented local trades

This audience does not primarily buy software.
They buy:
- reliability
- reassurance
- order
- continuity
- reduced drop-off
- fewer lost opportunities between incoming contact and real response

The viewer should feel:
- this is exactly what my day looks like
- this is exactly where requests get lost today
- this is exactly where tension starts
- this system seems to catch that exact moment

At the same time, the viewer should implicitly feel the customer perspective:
- if I call them, I am not left hanging
- I receive a sign of response
- I do not need to call several more businesses immediately

---

## 3. What this video must be
The video must feel:
- calm
- premium
- Swiss
- restrained
- precise
- emotionally clear
- serious
- mobile-first
- understandable without sound

The video must never feel like:
- a product demo
- a feature montage
- a UI gallery
- a slideshow
- a SaaS explainer
- a trades commercial
- a generic brand film
- a futuristic tech ad

---

## 4. Format targets
### Duration
- target: **13–14 seconds**
- minimum: 12 seconds
- maximum: 15 seconds

### Format strategy
The video must be designed as a **mobile-first master composition**.

From that master, produce:
- **Desktop / Landscape**
- **Mobile / Portrait**

### Safe area
All important action must happen in the **central image axis**.
No critical storytelling at the far edges.

---

## 5. Dramatic structure
This is **one single cinematic flow**, not multiple separate clips.

Internal states:
1. **Entry** — something relevant comes in while nobody is free
2. **Catch / reassurance** — the contact does not break, the customer is not left hanging
3. **Control / continuity** — the business stays in flow, the case is orderly captured, calm is restored

These must not feel like separate chapters.
They must feel like one controlled transformation.

---

## 6. World, materiality, light

### World
- real mobile trades work moment
- not office
- not desk
- not meeting room
- not showroom
- not polished industrial promo imagery

### Suggested environments
- plumbing service context
- installation context
- van-adjacent work environment
- compact mobile work setup
- realistic field-service atmosphere

### Materiality
- tools
- pipe / fitting / installation
- metal
- work fabric
- a real smartphone
- used but premium surfaces

Everything must feel real.
Not sterile.
Not overdesigned.
Not stock-like.

### Light
- calm morning or daytime light
- soft, controlled, premium
- dark blue tonal base
- subtle warm edge light
- no hard ad-style drama

### Style
- Swiss
- restrained
- premium
- precise
- calm
- serious
- modern without trendiness

---

## 7. Hard no-gos
The model must never generate:

- ad energy
- stock-footage feeling
- smiling faces to camera
- posing tradespeople
- heroic face shots
- exaggerated Hollywood drama
- frantic motion
- futuristic holograms
- sci-fi interfaces
- playful colorful app energy
- overloaded KPI dashboards
- slideshow feeling
- feature montage
- many hard cuts
- many location changes
- obvious AI-video aesthetics
- generic trades commercial imagery

Rule:
If the shot could also work for a generic hardware/tools/trades advertisement, it is wrong.

---

## 8. Visual through-line
The through-line is **not**:
- the person
- the workshop
- the product
- the app
- the dashboard

The through-line is:

> **An incoming contact gets caught even though nobody is free right now.**

Everything in the video must serve that truth.

---

## 9. Exact final scene progression

## 9.1 Second 0.0–3.5
### Hands full / incoming contact

Start immediately in the middle of a real work moment.

Show:
- hands of a technician or worker
- real trades activity
- tool / pipe / installation / material
- a smartphone in realistic proximity
- no face as the main subject

The phone must exist naturally within the work context:
- on a ledge
- within reach
- in a pocket
- on a mobile surface

Then the incoming contact happens:
- vibration
- slight screen wake-up
- incoming call / contact signal

Not loud.
Not hysterical.
But clearly relevant.

### Intended effect
The viewer instantly understands:
- this is real work
- nobody is free right now
- something relevant is coming in

---

## 9.2 Second 3.5–7.5
### Catch / the customer is not left hanging

This is the most important part of the entire hero video.

The video must make emotionally clear:
- the contact is not missed
- the customer is not dropped
- the system takes over in a trustworthy way

Important:
Do not explain the system technically.

Do not show:
- full voice agent sequence
- full app flow
- full wizard
- long text
- UI explanation

Instead show:
- one elegant, believable takeover moment
- the feeling that “we are already handling this”
- a brief premium cue of capture / reassurance / acknowledgement

### Intended effect
The viewer must feel:

> **The customer is not left hanging.**

---

## 9.3 Second 7.5–11.0
### Orderly capture / controlled guidance

Now the system may become visible very briefly as orderly reality.

Not as a product showcase.
Not as a feature tour.
Not as a dashboard demonstration.

Allowed:
- a new case exists
- clear structure
- calm mobile guidance
- minimal overview
- a small hint of capture / reassurance / status
- perhaps a subtle sense of dispatch or mobile case handling

Not allowed:
- too many buttons
- multiple screen swaps
- full case details
- long readable text
- KPI parade
- detailed dashboard obsession

### Intended effect
The viewer does not need to read everything.
The viewer must feel:

> **This is captured. This is under control.**

---

## 9.4 Second 11.0–14.0
### Control / continuity / calm

The scene gently returns toward the real work moment.

The worker continues calmly:
- focused
- steady
- composed
- not smiling
- not posing
- not triumphant
- not commercial

A small glance toward the phone is optional, not required.

### Intended effect
The viewer feels:
- the business remains operational
- the contact is not lost
- calm has been restored
- the work continues
- the business stays in flow

### Closing feeling
Not:
- triumph
- ad finale
- product victory

But:

> **Everything continues — just more cleanly.**

---

This hero video must **not** try to include:

- full dispatch hub

- entry

## 11. Camera direction
### Allowed
- slow approach
- minimal controlled movement
- soft focus shift
- organic transitions
- one single premium movement flow

### Forbidden
- aggressive push-ins
- frantic reframing
- music-video rhythm
- action-cam energy
- many perspective changes
- visible clip structure

---

## 12. Editing direction
### Goal
The piece must feel like **one single cinematic stream**.

### Therefore
- use as few hard cuts as possible
- prefer transitions through motion, focus, reflections, device, light, depth of field

### Forbidden
- slideshow feeling
- asset parade
- chapter-card structure
- screen gallery
- stacked UI presentation

---

## 13. Quality-control checklist
Before every render, all of these must be true:

1. This is a real trades moment, not an ad.
2. It is clear nobody is free right now.
3. It is clear the contact still gets caught.
4. The video tells reassurance, not product.
5. The customer perspective is emotionally present.
6. The world stays calm, premium, Swiss, restrained.
7. The system is embedded, not displayed.
8. The shot is understandable without sound.
9. The result would still feel premium in 2028.
10. The ending creates calm instead of demo energy.

If any answer is no, the shot is not good enough.

## 15. FINAL COPY-PASTE PROMPT FOR GEMINI VEO

Create a 12–15 second ultra-premium homepage hero video for a Swiss software brand serving local trades businesses. The video must feel like one single calm cinematic masterpiece and must never feel like a product demo, feature montage, slideshow, UI gallery, SaaS explainer, generic trades commercial, or stock-footage ad.

The central truth of the video is: even when nobody in the business can answer the phone right now, the customer is not left hanging. Their issue is captured, they receive reassurance, and the business stays in flow.

The scene takes place in a real mobile trades work moment, not in an office, not at a desk, and not in a showroom. Show a believable work context with real materiality: hands working, tools, pipe, metal, fabric, a slightly used smartphone, and a real mobile work environment. The face must not be the main subject and must not be staged like a commercial actor.

The video must begin immediately in the middle of a real work moment. The worker’s hands are visibly occupied with a real trades task. A smartphone nearby signals an incoming contact or call. It must become instantly clear that nobody is free to answer right now. There must be no exaggerated stress acting, no alarm aesthetics, and no frantic drama.

Then the video must elegantly, calmly, and credibly show that the contact is not lost. The worker does not react frantically. Instead, the system takes over in a quiet, trustworthy, and reassuring way. This is the most important moment in the entire video. It must become emotionally clear that the customer is not left hanging. Do not explain the system technically. Do not show a full app flow, a full wizard, a full voice-agent sequence, or a product demonstration. Show only one brief, premium, believable takeover moment that makes the viewer feel that the contact is already being handled.

After that, the video may briefly reveal that the incoming contact has become an orderly case. Show only minimal structured system truth: a new case, calm order, clear mobile guidance, a subtle sense of overview and capture. Do not show long readable text, detailed dashboard numbers, KPI panels, too many buttons, full case details, or a feature tour.

At the end, the video must gently return toward the real work moment. The worker continues calmly and stays in flow. No smiling to camera, no posing, no heroic energy, no advertising tone. The final emotional effect must be: everything continues, but now more cleanly and under control.

The visual style must be Swiss, premium, restrained, precise, calm, controlled, and serious. Use deep dark blues, real materiality, subtle warm edge light, and realistic surfaces. The camera must remain calm, expensive, fluid, and precise, with as few hard cuts as possible. Keep all important action in the central image axis so that both desktop and mobile crops work cleanly. The video must be instantly understandable without sound.

Strictly forbidden: stock-footage feeling, commercial ad energy, smiling faces to camera, hero poses, sci-fi interfaces, holographic overlays, frantic cutting, slideshow feeling, feature montage, generic trades branding imagery, visible AI-video aesthetics, sterile overly perfect design world, music-video energy, KPI dashboards, product-demo tone, office scenes, desk scenes, futuristic tech-world aesthetics, exaggerated drama, or artificial hand acting.
